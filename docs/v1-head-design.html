The Pz residual head: where the error lives, and how v1 predicts it

A measurement — every closure error on cal_18 lives in the laterally-corrugated shell of the cavity — and the head design it licenses: an envelope taken from the live cavity times a small learned shape factor. No interface convention (zif) anywhere.

1Why the residual can only live in the corrugated shell

The learned object sits in the offset slot of the polarization law ⟨Pz⟩ = A·⟨ℰz⟩ + Poff, and Poff is a lateral covariance: how "some of this plane is water, some is not" correlates with the lateral field pattern. Two hard locality facts follow.

Support. A plane that is all water (bulk) or all cavity/metal has no lateral χ fluctuation — the covariance is identically zero there. Cauchy–Schwarz makes this an inequality: |Poff(z)| ≤ √varxy(χ) · √varxy(ℰz).

Reach. A lateral field pattern of wavelength λ decays as e−2πd/λ with distance d: molecular-scale patterns (λ ≈ 3 Å) from atoms more than ~2 Å away are exponentially gone. Distant atoms contribute only the plane mean, which belongs to A·⟨ℰ⟩, not to the covariance.

So the residual at height z is set by the atoms within a ~2 Å shell of that height — the outermost water layer and the top metal layer. Both statements are measurable.

3The parametrization: envelope × shape factor

v1 residual
ΔP(z) = Gσz ∗ [ w(z) · Σk ck Bk(u(z)) ]K = 8;  σz = 0.2 Å
w(z) = √varxy[χ] · √varxy[ℰscr]envelope — where a residual may exist; physical units, not normalized
u(z) = Σz′≤z w ⁄ Σ winterface progress, 0 → 1 across the shell
Bk(u) = e−(u−uk)²/2σ²,  uk = (k−½)/K,  σ = 1/Kfixed basis — never changes
ck = 0.25 · tanh( MLP(xk) )the only learned numbers (§5)
Gσz: fixed Gaussian blur in zz-width floor — caps the ρb frequencies the u-compression would create (§4)

The hard, structure-dependent geometry — where the shell sits, how wide, how strong — is carried by w and u, both computed from the cavity that is already built live every forward. Because w has physical units, interface roughness and the charge-state field amplitude act directly through the envelope; because w vanishes outside the shell, the residual cannot leak out, whatever the coefficients do. The basis only has to draw a smooth curve on a normalized [0, 1] interval.

End-to-end. The K = 8 reconstruction through the solve: φ 3.6×10⁻³ eV (prior-only 0.747; machinery floor 5.6×10⁻⁴). The σz floor exists because the progress coordinate squeezes mid-shell basis functions to ~0.7 Å in z and ρb amplifies the unsmoothed leftover into wiggles; 0.2 Å improves every metric at once, 0.4 Å over-smooths (φ 2×10⁻²).

The metric matters more than capacity. Measured leverage: a unit-integral Poff error costs −159 eV of electrode potential at the shell's metal edge but −1.4 eV on the screened solvent side (>100× — dielectric screening). Plain L2 is blind to this weight: over K = 6…32, φ bounces non-monotonically (3.6×10⁻³–6.5×10⁻², each value predicted to ~1.4× by the leverage-weighted moment of the leftover) and no K beats 8. The φ-optimal eight coefficients (Gauss–Newton — the simulation of converged v1) reach φ 2.3×10⁻³ at a mild ρb cost (peak 7.2→8.7×10⁻⁴): a fixed information budget; φ-training parks the deficit in pointwise ρb, the one channel nothing in the pipeline reads. v2's label regression must be leverage-weighted, not plain L2.

What stays sharp in ρb is the target's own fine structure. A ρb-metric-optimal fit gains 1.3%; an atom-anchored basis (110 dof) at most 1.6× with unlearnable coefficients; grid refinement (dz 0.091→0.046) changes nothing, while coarsening aliases (at 0.18 Å even the exact anchor degrades). Recovering it needs the 3-D field itself. The residual right of the band is the absorption tail — outside the envelope's reach, but its φ effect is an offset the in-band coefficients cancel (the φ-optimal run does exactly that). All tunings are cal_18; the label factory revalidates them for free.

5Predicting the coefficients

ck — explicit
ck = 0.25 · tanh( MLP( [ Fk ‖ Fglob ‖ sk ] ) )one MLP shared by all zones
Fk = Σi ωik fi ⁄ Σi ωikzone-k pooled atom features (1152)
ωik = w(zi) · e−(u(zi)−uk)²/2σf²,  σf = 1.5/Ksoft atom weights: in the shell × near zone k
Fglob = (1/N) Σi fiwhole-structure mean (1152)
sk = [ qtot/10,  uk,  Pprior(uk)/0.3 ]three scalars
MLP: 2307 → 128 → 1SiLU; final layer zero-init → ΔP ≡ 0 at step 0

fi are the per-atom multi-layer mixed features the center-head already uses (layer_feature_mixer; each encodes the atom's ~10 Å environment). The two poolings differ only in weights: Fk answers "what does slice k look like" — only shell atoms near uk speak; Fglob answers "what kind of system is this" — the context the normalized zone mean has discarded. The weights are soft: a shell oxygen at u = 0.30 contributes ≈ 0.80 to zone 3, ≈ 0.68 to zone 2, ≈ 0.31 to zone 5; a deep slab atom (w ≈ 0) is silent everywhere. No hard partition → features move smoothly with atoms and stay differentiable, which the end-to-end v1 gradient requires.

Deliberately excluded, and why: absolute z, cell size, ze50 (replaced by u); species counts (already in fi); A(uk) (monotone across the shell — duplicates uk); ⟨Sion⟩(uk) (the ion cavity opens outside the shell, ≈0 over most of it). The last two are ablation candidates once v2 labels exist; in the label-free v1 every input is a potential fudge channel, so the scalar set stays minimal.

6One forward pass

Every step encodes one physical judgment: where a residual may exist (the cavity decides), who gets a say (shell atoms), what they say (local arrangement → local correction). Nothing is supervised in v1: the potential / Fermi / Φ1D loss gradients flow back through the differentiable 1-D solve into the MLP and the MACE trunk. The energy/forces path stays detached from the head at first.

7Trainability, and the risks

  • The gradient signal is strong and already measured. The medium screens an offset-slot bias ~10× in Pz; the survivor lands almost entirely as a slab-side potential shift (~2×10⁻² of offset ↦ ~1.2 eV of electrode potential). The potential and Fermi losses see the head at first order.
  • Risk: shape under-determination. The observables mostly constrain the integrated moment of ΔP — shape drift is possible (the layer_mean-creep failure mode of the planar era). Monitor per epoch: rms, dipole moment, correlation with the prior. v2 is the antidote if it bites; v1-vs-v2 is the fudge experiment.
  • Risk: the envelope follows the model density. Early in training the density is poor and the envelope with it. Mitigations: zero-init (ΔP ≡ 0 at step 0) and the per-structure health guard (Newton failure → prior-only fallback).
  • Single-structure evidence. All numbers — including the σz = 0.2 Å sweet spot, which sits in a narrow window (0.4 Å already collapses φ) — are cal_18; the cross-structure check rides along with the label factory.
