1Architecture

The planar Gaussian compensation layer is replaced by a per-structure nonlinear PB solve (--solvent_model pb). The laterally averaged solvent charge — ionic plus bound polarization — enters the model through the same three hook points the planar layer used: SCF field features, the periodic 1-D compensation energy, and the potential observable. The profile is detached (lagged self-consistency); at convergence of training this coincides with the self-consistent solution.

The solver is fully device-resident (float64, half-spectrum FFTs). The model's net density is assembled spectrally — structure factors × GTO form factors — replacing pointwise evaluation: 16 s → ~1 ms per structure, exact to the Nyquist floor. The grid is 0.15 Å (100×100×300) with the bound charge included in the layer, fixed by a cell-dependent convergence study on real training cells. fixsol=5 dipole self-consistency steps are used after measuring a ~0.14 V protocol offset of fixsol=2 on real structures.

2Exact DFT baseline cache

The PB cavity and the solute potential need the total electron density and the solute electrostatic potential — not just the model's residual density. Instead of Gaussian surrogates, a one-time cache stores per-structure exact fields from the training DFT: the neutral valence density and phi_base = Hartree(neutral) + exact local pseudopotential. At runtime the solute potential is phi_base − Hartree(net_model), exact by Poisson linearity.

  • Memory-mapped [400, 3, 100, 100, 300] float32, 14.4 GB — ranks share pages.
  • Five per-structure verifications (positions, electron count, positivity, cross-checks): 400/400 pass.
  • End-to-end spot check reproduces the grid-convergence anchor to 0.003 V.
  • The Gaussian surrogates it replaces differed by 0.25–0.4 V in the solvent potential term.

3Schedule and guards

Solving PB for every structure at every step is unnecessary. The schedule: 50 planar warm-up encounters per structure, then a fresh PB solve every 5th encounter with the cached profile reused in between (detached, so reuse is gradient-safe). A static-shard sampler pins each structure to one rank, aligning encounters with epochs and keeping the warm-start cache local (see the attribution report for a measured accuracy trade-off of this choice).

A per-structure health guard falls back to the planar layer if a solve is unhealthy (residual > 10×tol, layer outside the cell, runaway bound dipole) — self-deactivating as the model converges. A φ warm-start cache (healthy solves only) makes refresh solves ~2× cheaper than cold ones. The warm-up matters: at 50 encounters the planar→PB transition is seamless (validation loss 0.334 → 0.430, forces still improving); at 10 encounters the loss jumped 4×; with no warm-up at all, early training diverged.

5Outcome

The production run finished with zero unhealthy solves in 43,961 — the guard never fired. End-of-run validation: E 2.8 meV/atom, F 21.7 meV/Å, potential 0.067 eV, Φ1D 0.067 eV.

Against the 9-final baselines (same data, 400 epochs each): the PB run's Fermi-level error ends well below the original CP-MACE baseline (0.066 vs 0.109 eV) and above the fully-supervised planar run (0.034 eV) — the attribution report (2/3) accounts for that remaining gap.
